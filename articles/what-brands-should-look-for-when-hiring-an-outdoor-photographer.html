Hiring an Outdoor Photographer

What Brands Should Look for When Hiring an Outdoor Photographer

A good outdoor photographer needs to do more than take sharp photos.

A good outdoor photographer needs to do more than take sharp photos.

They need to understand the setting, the gear, the people, and the story your brand is trying to tell.

Start by looking at their work.

Does it feel real? Do the people look like they belong there? Is the gear being used the right way? Can they shoot action, wide landscapes, small details, products, and people?

Then look beyond the portfolio.

Can they work in rough weather? Are they able to hike, travel, carry gear, and keep up with the people they are photographing? Do they understand that a good outdoor shoot may start before sunrise and end long after the light is gone?

You should also ask whether they understand commercial work.

A great landscape photographer is not always a great brand photographer. Commercial outdoor photography has to serve a purpose. The photos may need to work for ads, websites, social media, catalogs, emails, dealers, and print.

That means the photographer should think about things like image shape, blank space for text, brand colors, product details, and how the content will be used later.

Most of all, look for someone who understands your audience.

Outdoor customers can spot fake moments quickly.

The right photographer should know when to step in, when to give direction, and when to get out of the way and let something real happen.

The goal is not just to make your brand look good.

It is to make your brand feel true.
