Outdoor Brand Checklist

The Ultimate Outdoor Brand Photoshoot Checklist

A good outdoor photoshoot starts long before the camera comes out.

A good outdoor photoshoot starts long before the camera comes out.

The better the plan, the more useful content you can create in one day.

First, know what you need the photos to do. Are you launching a product? Building a new website? Filling a social media calendar? Creating ads? Showing how a piece of gear works?

Then make a clear list of the products that matter most.

Do not bring twenty items and hope everything gets covered. Pick your hero products first. Know what must be shown, what details matter, and how the gear is meant to be used.

Next, think about location.

The setting should fit the product. Hunting gear belongs in hunting country. Fishing gear belongs near or in the water. Workwear should look like it is being worked in.

You should also think through:

  • Models who know how to use the gear
  • Clothing and colors
  • Weather
  • Time of day
  • Props and vehicles
  • Safety needs
  • Brand logos
  • Vertical and horizontal photos
  • Close-ups, wide shots, and action

The best outdoor brand photoshoots have structure, but they also leave room for real moments.

Maybe the wind kicks up. Maybe a dog takes off running. Maybe the sun breaks through at the right time.

Do not plan so tightly that you miss what is happening right in front of you.

Good planning gets you the shots you need.

Being ready gets you the shots you did not know you needed.
